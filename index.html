Information and Help

General Information

In the star map (which is in equatorial coordinates), north is on the top and east is on the left with 0 right ascension being the right edge as according to astronomical convention. The straight line in the middle of the map is the celestial equator and the curved line is the ecliptic. The size of the dots on the map correspond to apparent magnitude.

Generator Information

The generator only generates naked-eye (i.e. apparent magnitude < 6.5) stars.

  • Number of Stars - Number of stars that will be generated.
  • Location Seed 1 and 2 - Seeds that control the location of the stars.
  • Brightness Seed - Seed that controls the apparent magnitude of the stars.
  • Bias - Adjusts the brightness of the stars, larger (more positive) value makes stars brighter.
  • Precession - Increases the ecliptic longitude for every star. Makes stars adjust position. (Units: deg)
  • Axial Tilt - Axial tilt of the planet. Adjusts the ecliptic. (Units: deg)
  • NGP Ecl. Long. and Lat. - Ecliptic longitude and latitude of the North Galactic Pole (NGP). Adjust these to adjust the position of the "Milky Way". (Units: deg)
  • Galactic Bias - Adjusts the sharpness of the galaxy, with 0 being completely random star distribution, and 1 being completely concentrated star distribution.
